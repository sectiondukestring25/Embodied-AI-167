## Recommended Books

From classic theory to frontier practice, the books below are foundational for building a strong knowledge system:

<a id="books-table" name="books-table"></a>
| Title | Author | Overview | Why It Matters |
| :---- | :----- | :------- | :------------- |
| **Introduction to Robotics** | John J. Craig | A classic robotics textbook covering spatial descriptions, kinematics, dynamics, and trajectory planning. | Essential reading for understanding the geometry and motion relationships that underlie robotic systems. |
| **Modern Robotics** | Kevin M. Lynch, Frank C. Park | A systematic treatment of robot kinematics, dynamics, and control built from screw theory. | A more modern presentation than traditional textbooks, especially suitable for readers interested in mathematical derivations. |
| **Reinforcement Learning (2nd Edition)** | Richard S. Sutton | The canonical text in reinforcement learning, covering everything from core algorithms to deep RL theory. | A must-read for mastering how agents learn decision-making through interaction. |
| **Deep Learning** | Ian Goodfellow | A foundational work in deep learning covering core theory and mainstream model families. | Essential background for building perception modules such as vision and language. |
| **Computer Vision: Models, Learning, and Inference** | Simon J.D. Prince | A systematic introduction to probabilistic models and machine learning methods in computer vision. | Provides strong mathematical grounding for embodied systems that process visual input. |
| **Probabilistic Robotics** | Sebastian Thrun | A detailed treatment of probabilistic methods for robot localization, mapping, and SLAM. | A key reference for handling uncertainty in the real world. |
| **Visual SLAM in Fourteen Lectures: From Theory to Practice** | Gao Xiang, Zhang Tao, et al. | A practical Chinese-language guide covering 3D geometry, optimization, visual odometry, loop closure, and back-end optimization. | One of the most practical bridges into SLAM for Chinese-speaking learners. |
| **Human-Robot Interaction: An Introduction** | Christoph Bartneck | A structured introduction to HRI, including ethics, cognition, and social dimensions. | Crucial for understanding how robots collaborate with people in real-world applications. |
| **Mechanics of Robotic Manipulation** | Matthew T. Mason | A classic text that dives deeply into the mechanics of robotic manipulation. | Focuses on manipulation as a core capability and is especially valuable for advanced study. |
| **Robotics, Vision and Control** | Peter Corke | Combines robotics, computer vision, and control using MATLAB/Simulink as a practical toolchain. | Includes many code examples and is well suited to rapid prototyping and experimentation. |
| **Springer Handbook of Robotics** | Bruno Siciliano, Oussama Khatib | An encyclopedia-style handbook covering mechanisms, perception, planning, control, HRI, and applications. | Useful both for building a panoramic map of the field and for looking up standard references in specific topics. |
| **Rigid Body Dynamics Algorithms** | Roy Featherstone | A systematic treatment of rigid-body dynamics algorithms, spatial vector representations, and efficient dynamics computation. | Fills in the dynamics backbone behind legged robots, humanoids, and manipulators. |
| **Planning Algorithms** | Steven M. LaValle | A comprehensive treatment of discrete planning, motion planning, planning under uncertainty, and differential constraints. | Covers the planning methodology that is often overlooked in embodied AI. |
| **Algorithms for Decision Making** | Mykel J. Kochenderfer | A unified textbook spanning probabilistic inference, sequential decision-making, POMDPs, RL, and multi-agent decision processes. | Ideal for viewing perception uncertainty, control, and learning within a single framework. |

> 💡 **Tip**: You do not need to read every book cover to cover. Start with one foundation text, such as *Modern Robotics*, and use the others as references when needed.
